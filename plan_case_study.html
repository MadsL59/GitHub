Plan Case Study

Save Now to Experience Later

Saving money can be a tricky task to do, considering we live in a world where we are bombarded with ads every day but also when we are constrained by time. But what do you do when you need that extra cash to get what you want? Probably not what everyone would like to hear, but, YES, you start SAVING. Pen and paper are out of the question (hello 2021!). I wanted to make an app that comes to everyone’s rescue, an app that shows how much you spend and on what but also provides an insight into what you can do to cut costs and save money in a certain amount of time. Yes, there are a lot of apps like that out there, but not a lot are fun, engaging and reliable like PLAN.

My Role

My role as a UI/UX designer for this project involved doing user research, testing and a competitor analysis, user flow diagrams, wireframes, protyping and high-fidelity mock-ups as well as branding and visual design. This app was part of a UI Course I took from CareerFoundry, online.

Wish, Plan and Accomplish

You can take advantage of the Open Banking platform to safely retrieve information on your actual finances and get personalized advice on how to reach your savings goals. The world of Finance can be daunting for many of us who are less familiar with the industry, but PLAN's main goal is to be reliable, clear and to build confidence in all of its users. All data on income and expenses can be recorded easily and on the go on the app and it is accessible at all times with a viable internet connection.

Challenge accepted

With so many similar apps out there, the challenge was to create a fun and youthful app that would appeal to the majority of users but also that would add value in their lives. The Finance world can be scary and not everyone knows and understands the HOW TOs so the challenge was not to make ANOTHER money saving app but one that gets the attention of users and makes them come back and actually use it. So, mixing FUN with SAFETY and RELIABILITY was the real challenge when designing the app.

Are you using the app?

PLAN would work for people who want to save money or find difficulty in saving money, people in their 20s with small income, people who want to make a big purchase in a limited amount of time. Is that YOU?

Research

I started the process by downloading a lot of money saving apps to get a feel of what’s out there and what features these apps have. What did my competitor analysis found? I looked at Emma, Moneybox and Monzo and they provided valuable insight into what users would expect from this type of product and the main featurs my app should cover.Then, keeping in mind the prospective users and the fact that PLAN should be clear and straight-forward, I created potential USER STORIES followed by the final USER FLOW DIAGRAM using Whimsical.

Wireframes

Scenario

Alex wants to go travelling for a year and needs to start saving but he doesn’t know what the best approach is. He is looking for a fun app that is simple and does the most work for him and can tell him exactly what his financial situation is and what he can do to achieve his goal.

Branding

I was also responsible for branding, so I created brand guidelines and a logo for this project. I chose yellow and orange as the overall chromatic because both are happy colours and create good energy and a fun, relaxed vibe. The logo’s caligraphic style is meant to replicate the idea of writing down notes in a notebook, using pen and paper as if the user is taking notes of spendings, creating budgets and making plans.

Testing and Results

After finishing with my high-fidelity wireframes, I created a prototype and tested it on 3 users based on given scenarios. The main issue that was raised was the Dashboard/Overview Screen.Testers said the screen is not clear enough, they didn’t know exactly how to access the spendings overview or categories, nor what the yellow button’s function was. Also, users pointed out that after reading the headline, they would be inclined to look at the graph to see how close they are to their goals but in fact, the graph was showing an overview of their expenditure. All these issues seemed to confuse all users that tested the app.

Solution

Redesigned the dashboard screen so it’s clear and straight-forward

Added coach marks so users know what key features do

Retrospect

I am really happy with the final result and how the app looks like including the colour palette, illustrations and overall vibe. The project was reviewed by my peers, my tutor and mentor and I received very good feedback which was overall about how I successfully managed to make a moneysaving/fintech app look exciting and not plain and boring as most apps on the market.

In terms of what didn’t go well, the project was done in a short period of time which meant less testing on users in the early stages. Also, because I wanted the app to be simple and not to overcomplicate features, the early design stages were a bit difficult too in terms of visuals which led to having a creative block. Furthermore, not thinking about overboarding screens or just coach marks to tell users how to use the app was a big disadvantage and the problem was spotted by all users that tested the final prototype.

What I would improve? Allowing more time to do usability testing and a more comprehensive competitor analysis to enhance the features of the app and the overall experience. I would also explore more screens, and any other smart ways of using the app as well as focus on onboarding to faciliate and to make the user journey easier and more practical.
